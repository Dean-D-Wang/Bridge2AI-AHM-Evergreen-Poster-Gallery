Hey, this is Yu Yan. I am the first author of this presentation. Welcome to my poster titled “MIND-S: A Deep-learning Prediction Model for Elucidating Protein Post-translational Modifications in Human Diseases.”
Protein post-translational modifications, or PTMs, are chemical changes to proteins that occur after they are synthesized. These modifications are essential for regulating protein function and are widely involved in many biological processes and disease mechanisms. However, identifying and understanding PTMs remains challenging, as experimental approaches are often costly, labor-intensive, and time-consuming.
Computational methods have emerged as an efficient alternative for predicting PTM sites. Most existing approaches focus on short amino acid sequences surrounding a target site. While useful, these methods often overlook global protein context and require different parameter settings for different PTM types, which limits their flexibility and scalability.
Another key challenge is interpretability. Many deep learning models achieve strong predictive performance but function as black boxes, making it difficult to understand the biological basis of their predictions.
To address these challenges, we developed MIND-S, a deep-learning-based platform for PTM prediction at the protein level. MIND-S integrates both protein sequence and structural information, allowing it to analyze entire proteins rather than just local regions. It leverages a multi-head attention mechanism and a graph neural network, combined in a 15-fold ensemble model, to simultaneously predict multiple types of PTMs with high accuracy and computational efficiency.
Importantly, MIND-S includes an interpretation module based on integrated gradients. This module identifies which amino acids are most important for each prediction, providing biological insight and enabling the discovery of PTM-related patterns without prior knowledge. These interpretations align with known motifs and can also reveal previously unknown patterns.
We applied MIND-S to 26 types of PTMs across datasets of approximately 50,000 proteins. The model demonstrates strong predictive performance and scalability. In addition, MIND-S can evaluate how genetic mutations, such as single nucleotide polymorphisms, affect PTMs, helping bridge the gap between genomic variation and protein regulation.
Overall, MIND-S provides an accurate, interpretable, and efficient framework for studying PTMs and offers new opportunities to better understand protein regulation in health and disease.
Our work, presented in this poster, was published in both Cell Reports Methods and Star Protocols in 2023. You can access the code for MIND-S on our GitHub repository by scanning the QR code on my poster. Thank you for your attention.
